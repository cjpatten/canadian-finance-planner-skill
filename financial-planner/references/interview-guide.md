# Interview Guide

This reference contains the full interview question set. Read this when starting or resuming
an interview with a new user.

## Interview Principles

- Ask 2-3 questions at a time. Never dump a wall of questions.
- Acknowledge each answer with warmth before asking the next round.
- Explain WHY you're asking when it's not obvious ("I ask about your province because tax
  brackets vary significantly — Ontario vs. Alberta is a big difference").
- If they upload documents, extract financial data silently. Don't narrate what you're ignoring.
- Save progress to `1-my-profile.md` after each round.
- If someone says "I don't know" — help them figure it out or note it as estimated.

## Suggesting Documents

Early in the interview, mention what would be helpful to have on hand (but don't make it
homework — they can upload anytime):

- CRA My Account data (RRSP room, TFSA room, Notice of Assessment)
- Pay stubs or T4 slips
- Bank and credit card statements (2-3 months)
- Mortgage statement
- Investment account statements (RRSP, TFSA, RESP, non-registered)
- Employer benefits package summary
- Insurance policies
- Any loan/debt documents

If they can't or won't gather these, work with whatever they give you and flag which numbers
are estimated vs. confirmed.

## Round 1: Who Are You? (Life Context)

This shapes everything — taxes, benefits, insurance, estate.

**Questions:**
- Name and age (and partner's age if applicable)
- Province or territory
- Relationship status: single / married / common-law / separated / divorced / widowed
  - If partnered: first marriage/partnership? (blended family implications)
- Children: how many, ages?
  - Any with disabilities or special needs? (RDSP eligibility)
  - Any from a previous relationship? (support obligations)
  - Adult children still dependent?
- Supporting aging parents financially?
- Housing: own, rent, live with family, social housing?
- Employment: full-time / part-time / contract / self-employed / gig / unemployed / disability / retired / student
  - Public or private sector? Union?
  - Income stability: steady, variable, unpredictable?
- Employer benefits? (health/dental, life insurance, disability, pension, RRSP match)
- Workplace pension? (Defined benefit / defined contribution / neither)
- Canadian citizen or permanent resident? (OAS eligibility: 10+ years for partial, 40 for full)
- Health conditions affecting financial planning?
- Currently receiving government benefits? (social assistance, disability, EI, workers' comp, CPP-D, GIS)

**Why each matters:**
- Province → tax brackets, provincial benefits programs
- Relationship → filing options, income splitting, survivor benefits, spousal RRSP
- Children → CCB, RESP/CESG, child care deduction, dependent credits
- Special needs → RDSP, DTC, Henson Trust, long-term planning
- Housing → mortgage strategy vs rent optimization
- Employment type → CPP contributions, EI eligibility, tax planning
- Pension → retirement income layer, reduces need for personal savings
- Residency duration → OAS partial vs full
- Government benefits → clawback considerations, transition planning

## Round 2: What Do You Earn? (All Income Sources)

**Employment income:**
- Gross annual salary/wages
- Net take-home pay (monthly or biweekly)
- Commissions, bonuses, overtime (average and range)
- Partner's income (same breakdown)

**Self-employment / side hustles:**
- Gross revenue, business expenses, net profit
- Incorporated or sole proprietorship?
- HST/GST registered?

**Government benefits currently received:**
- CCB (monthly amount)
- GST/HST credit (quarterly)
- Provincial benefits (Ontario Trillium, BC Climate Action, etc.)
- Social assistance / welfare
- EI, CPP retirement/disability, OAS/GIS, workers' comp, ODSP or equivalent

**Other income:**
- Rental income (gross and net)
- Investment income (dividends, interest, capital gains)
- Pension income
- Support received (child or spousal)
- Any other sources

**Calculate after this round:**
- Total household gross and net income
- Income stability score
- Marginal and average tax rates (federal + provincial)
- Whether quarterly installments are required

## Round 3: What Goes Out? (Every Dollar)

**Fixed monthly:**
- Housing: mortgage/rent, property tax, condo fees, home insurance, maintenance
- Transport: car payment, auto insurance, gas, parking, transit
- Debt minimums: credit cards, LOC, student loans, personal loans, CRA debt
- Insurance premiums (personal, not through employer)
- Childcare / daycare
- Support payments (child or spousal)
- Phone, internet, streaming/subscriptions (list each)
- Regular savings auto-contributions (RRSP, TFSA, RESP already set up)
- Professional dues, union fees
- Education costs, pet expenses

**Variable monthly:**
- Groceries (separate from dining out)
- Dining out / takeout / coffee
- Utilities (hydro, gas, water — seasonal average)
- Clothing, personal care
- Entertainment, hobbies
- Kids' activities and equipment
- Household supplies
- Health expenses not covered (dental, prescriptions, therapy, glasses)
- Gifts, donations
- Alcohol / cannabis / tobacco (non-judgmental — just need the number)
- Personal spending / fun money
- Travel (annualized)

**Annual / irregular (people always forget these):**
- Property tax (if not in mortgage), annual insurance premiums
- Vehicle maintenance, holiday spending, birthday gifts
- Back-to-school, summer camp, home repairs
- Professional development, tax prep fees, charitable donations

**Calculate after this round:**
- Total monthly expenses (fixed + variable + annualized irregular)
- Monthly surplus or deficit
- Savings rate (% of net income)
- Expense breakdown by category
- Compare to benchmarks: CMHC 32% GDS, 40% TDS, 50/30/20 rule
- Identify "joy spending" vs "habit spending" (ask which categories bring most happiness)

## Round 4: What Do You Owe? (Every Debt)

For EACH debt:
- Type: credit card / LOC / HELOC / car loan / student loan / personal loan / mortgage / CRA debt / payday loan / family loan / BNPL / other
- Current balance
- Interest rate (APR), fixed or variable
- Minimum monthly payment
- Special terms (0% promo rate and expiry, student loan interest credit, secured vs unsecured)
- Joint or individual

**Specifically ask about:**
- Payday loans (these are emergencies — 300-500%+ effective interest)
- CRA tax debt (prescribed interest rate, payment arrangements)
- Buy-now-pay-later balances (Afterpay, Klarna, PayBright)
- Debts in collections, consumer proposal or bankruptcy history

**Calculate after this round:**
- Total consumer debt (excluding mortgage)
- Debt-to-income ratio
- Total monthly interest being paid
- Avalanche vs snowball ranking and payoff timelines
- Whether consolidation would help
- Flag any debts where minimum < monthly interest (mathematically unpayable)

## Round 5: What Do You Have? (Assets & Savings)

**Cash & savings:**
- Chequing and savings account balances (what rate?)
- Emergency fund — designated separately or mixed?

**Registered accounts (for each):**
- RRSP: balance, investments (cash/GICs/mutual funds/ETFs/stocks), employer match details, spousal RRSP?, HBP/LLP repayment?
- TFSA: balance, investments
- RESP: balance per child, investments, contributing $2,500/year per child for full CESG?
- FHSA: opened? balance? (only if first-time buyer)
- RDSP: if DTC eligible — balance, grants received (CDSG, CDSB)
- LIRA: locked-in pension from former employer
- DPSP: employer deferred profit sharing

**Non-registered:** Brokerage accounts, GICs, crypto holdings

**Real estate:** Primary residence (value, mortgage, equity), rental properties, cottage, land

**Other:** Business equity, vehicles (if owned), valuable property, expected inheritance

**Key numbers to determine:**
- RRSP contribution room (CRA My Account or estimate)
- TFSA contribution room (calculate from age + residency, or ask)
- RESP lifetime room remaining per child

**Calculate after this round:**
- Total liquid, invested, real estate equity, total assets
- Total liabilities
- Net worth (and net worth excluding primary residence)
- Investment allocation (% stocks, bonds, cash, real estate)
- Whether they're capturing all "free money" (employer match, CESG, RDSP grants)

## Round 6: What Protects You? (Insurance & Safety Nets)

**Through employer:**
- Extended health & dental (coverage level, family or individual?)
- Life insurance (how much — usually 1x or 2x salary)
- STD/LTD (coverage, elimination period)
- Critical illness, AD&D
- HSA/WSA (balance, reset date)

**Personal policies:**
- Life insurance (term or permanent, amount, premium)
- Disability insurance (own-occupation or any-occupation, benefit)
- Critical illness, home insurance, auto insurance, umbrella/liability

**For business owners:** Key person, business interruption, professional liability

**Assess:**
- Life insurance adequacy: 10-12x income if dependents? Mortgage payoff + income replacement + kids' education?
- Disability gap: 60-70% of income covered?
- If NO life insurance and they have dependents → flag as CRITICAL
- Will and Powers of Attorney in place?

---

## Round 7: What Do You Want? (Goals & Priorities)

**Open questions first:**
- What's the #1 thing you'd change about your money situation right now?
- What keeps you up at night about money?
- If money wasn't a concern, how would life look different?

**Short-term (0-2 years):** Pay off specific debt, build emergency fund, vacation, major purchase, catch up on RRSP/TFSA, open FHSA

**Medium-term (2-7 years):** Buy/upgrade home, fund kids' education, pay off mortgage faster, career change, start a business, major renovation

**Long-term (7+ years):** Retirement (what age? lifestyle? where?), financial independence, inheritance, help kids buy a home, travel lifestyle

**Lifestyle calibration:**
- What spending brings the most joy? (Protect this.)
- What spending is habit that you wouldn't miss? (Redirect this.)
- Subscriptions you should cancel?
- Debt stress level (1-10) → determines payoff aggressiveness
- Investment risk comfort (conservative / moderate / aggressive, or use scenario questions)
- Budget preference: strict or flexible?

## After the Interview

Once all 7 rounds are complete:
1. Save the final `1-my-profile.md` with all data
2. Read `references/calculations-and-dashboard.md`
3. Build the plan, budget, and dashboard
4. Present key insights conversationally
5. Highlight the single most impactful action they can take THIS WEEK
